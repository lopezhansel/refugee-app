About Us

LUTHERAN FAMILY SERVICES DENVER

The Refugee and Asylee Program is one of seven programs within Lutheran Family Services Rocky Mountains (LFS). The Denver refugee program is an affiliate of Lutheran Immigration and Refugee Services (LIRS) and Episcopal Migration Ministries (EMM). LIRS amd EMM are premier leaders among refugee resettlement agencies. The Refugee and Asylee Program is based on the east side of downtown Denver and maintains sub-offices in Colorado Springs, Greeley, Ft. Morgan, and Albuquerque, New Mexico.

Today more than 65 million refugees, asylum seekers, and internally displaced persons are living with a very uncertain future. The LFS Refugee & Asylee Programs are dedicated to helping these vulnerable individuals and families rebuild their lives by equipping them to achieve self-sufficiency within their first year in the United States and fostering integration into their new communities.

We cannot do our jobs without the support of volunteers. We rely heavily on local support – volunteers, churches, and community organizations – to accomplish our mission. We have various ways in which volunteers can get involved, with one of most popular mechanisms being our cultural mentoring program.
